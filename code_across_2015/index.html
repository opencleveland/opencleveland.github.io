Code Across Cleveland 2015

During the weekend of Feb. 20-22, we're participating in Code for Ameria's CodeAcross 2015 by hosting Code Across Cleveland. CodeAcross celebrates International Open Data Day.

What's Code Across?

A weekend of civic events across the country where technologists and community partners connect to tackle local challenges by adapting existing solutions and building new ones with technology and open data.

Events

Open House at Cleveland City Hall

On Friday, February 20 from 12 pm to 2 pm we are holding an open house in the library in Cleveland City Hall.

Code Across Cleveland

On Saturday, February 21 from 12 pm to 3 pm we will hold our Code Across Cleveland where we'll discuss what open data and civic apps can do for the residents of Cleveland and all of Northeast Ohio. Ideas we'll discuss:

• See a live demonstration of Large Lots, a website for people to find and buy City-owned vacant lot, streamlining the process.

• Create a searchable index of Cleveland City Council's legislation and updates.

• Use RTA's newly released real-time data to give up-to-date bus schedules.

• Add your own ideas for civic apps!

Learn: What's open data and civic hacking all about? Is Open Cleveland for you? If you have an idea of a project that can help improve a local government or have developed a civic app? If you're a local govt official or employee, share your experiences with data sharing in your workplace and find out how you can share the data that's available to you and promote open data policies there can help lower costs, streamline citizens' public requests, and more. So, come join developers, designers, data geeks, civic leaders, organizers, idea-makers, and anyone else who is interested to learn about our brigade. Programming experience is not necessary! A laptop is encouraged but not required. Open Cleveland is a Code For America Brigade.
